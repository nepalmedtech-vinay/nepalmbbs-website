NMC India — FMGL Regulations, 2021 Official

📋
NEET Mandatory

Per NMC Act 2019 and FMGL Regulations 2021, NEET is mandatory for Indian students wishing to practice medicine in India after MBBS abroad.

⏱️
Course Duration — Minimum 54 Months

Minimum 54 months (4.5 years) of study + 12 months internship at the same foreign institution. Total minimum: 5.5 years. Must complete within 10 years of joining.

🌐
English Medium Required

The medium of instruction must be English. Mandatory requirement for foreign medical graduates applying for Indian permanent registration.

🏥
12 Months India Supervised Internship

After completing foreign MBBS + 12-month foreign internship, graduates must complete a minimum 12-month supervised internship in India before applying for permanent registration.

📝
NExT Exam — Replacing FMGE

Graduates must clear the National Exit Test (NExT) conducted by NBE to obtain permanent registration. NExT is replacing FMGE for all graduates — Indian and foreign.

⚡
Grandfathering — Existing Students

Students who acquired their foreign degree OR were pursuing education before November 2021 are governed by earlier FMGE regulations, not the 2021 NExT regulations.

📚
Mandatory Subjects — Schedule I

Course must include: Community Medicine, General Medicine, Psychiatry, Paediatrics, Surgery, Anaesthesia, OBG, Orthopaedics, ENT, Ophthalmology, Dermatology, Emergency services.

Nepal Medical Council — Official Notice, Nov 2024 Official

⏱️
5.5 Years Minimum Duration

NMC Nepal recognises MBBS/BDS with minimum academic duration of 5.5 years, including compulsory 1-year internship.

🌐
English as Primary Medium

All medical colleges in Nepal conduct academic programs in English as the primary medium of instruction — officially confirmed by Nepal NMC.

📋
Provisional Registration During Internship

Nepal NMC provides provisional registration to all MBBS/BDS students during their internship at affiliated teaching hospitals. Equivalent to Nepalese citizens.

🌍
Foreign Students — Licensing After Graduation

After graduation, foreign nationals are eligible to take licensing examinations per NMC Nepal regulations. Upon passing, they obtain a licence equivalent to Nepalese citizens.

✅
No Good Standing Certificate Required

Nepal NMC licence procedure does NOT require a good standing certificate from the candidate's home country council — simplifying the process for Indian students.

Common Questions
Parents & Students — Top Questions Answered
Admission & Eligibility
Is NEET mandatory for Nepal MBBS?
+

Yes. Per NMC India FMGL Regulations 2021, NEET is mandatory for Indian students who wish to practice medicine in India after MBBS abroad. Minimum 50th percentile General/OBC; 40th percentile SC/ST. Verify at nmc.org.in

Is a Nepal MBBS degree valid in India?
+

Valid only from NMC India recognised colleges. After graduating, must clear NExT or FMGE (NBE) + complete 1-year supervised India internship to obtain licence to practice. Always verify your college at NMC College Search before applying.

How long is the MBBS course in Nepal?
+

5.5 years total — 4.5 years academic + 1 year compulsory internship at affiliated teaching hospital. Same structure as MBBS in India. Confirmed by Nepal Medical Council official notice (November 2024). Source: mec.gov.np

When do Nepal MBBS admissions open in 2025?
+

Typically July to October. MEC Nepal publishes official admission notices at mec.gov.np. Track at entrance.mec.gov.np

How do I verify if a Nepal college is NMC India recognised?
+

Search the specific college at NMC India College Search. This is the ONLY authoritative source. Do not rely on college claims or agent assurances.

Fees & Finance
What is the fee for Nepal MBBS?
+

Fees are regulated by MEC Nepal's official fee ceiling and vary by college. We share current official MEC fee structures during counseling. Verify at mec.gov.np/fees. Always pay directly to college only — never to agents.

Can I get an education loan for Nepal MBBS?
+

Indian banks including SBI and Bank of Baroda provide education loans for MBBS at NMC India recognised foreign colleges. NMC recognition letter required. Consult your bank directly for terms.

Career & Future
What is NExT / FMGE and is it difficult?
+

FMGE (Foreign Medical Graduate Examination) is conducted by NBE twice yearly. NExT is replacing FMGE for all graduates. Nepal's India-aligned curriculum helps preparation, but serious study is required. Info: natboard.edu.in

Can I do NEET PG after Nepal MBBS?
+

After clearing NExT/FMGE + completing 1-year supervised India internship, you may be eligible for NEET PG. Rules subject to NMC updates. Always verify current eligibility at nmc.org.in.

What internship is required after Nepal MBBS?
+

Two internships: 1) 1 year in Nepal at affiliated teaching hospital (Nepal NMC requirement, counted in 5.5 years). 2) Additional 1 year supervised internship in India after graduation (NMC India FMGL Regulations 2021). Both are mandatory.

Life & Safety
Do Indian students need a visa for Nepal?
+

No. Indian citizens do not require a visa for Nepal under the India-Nepal bilateral open border agreement. A valid Indian passport or government-issued photo ID is sufficient for entry.

Is Nepal safe for Indian students?
+

Nepal and India share historically close ties. Indian students are well-received. Top medical colleges provide on-campus security and separate hostels. We strongly recommend speaking directly with current students at your shortlisted college before deciding.

What is the monthly living cost in Nepal?
+

Approximately ₹15,000–25,000 per month covering hostel, food, transport, and personal expenses. Varies by college location and lifestyle. On-campus hostel living is typically the most affordable option.

What documents do I need to apply?
+

Required documents: 10th+12th marksheets (notarised), NEET scorecard, valid passport, medical fitness certificate, character certificate from school/college, passport-size photos. Apply via entrance.mec.gov.np.

Life in Nepal
What to Expect — Honest Picture
Realistic information to help students and parents make informed decisions.
🌡️

Weather

Kathmandu: 5–30°C. Pleasant spring/autumn. Cold winters (5–10°C). Monsoon June–August. Similar to North India.

🍛

Food

Indian food in most college hostels. Dal, roti, sabzi available. Indian restaurants in cities. Easy adjustment.

🏠

Hostel

On-campus hostels with separate blocks for male/female. 24/7 security, Wi-Fi, study halls at most colleges.

💰

Monthly Cost

Approx ₹15,000–25,000/month covering hostel, food, transport, personal. Varies by lifestyle.

📱

Connectivity

Indian SIM cards roam in Nepal. Affordable local SIMs available. Good internet at colleges and cities.

👥

Community

Large Indian student community at all major colleges. Cultural events, peer support, student associations.
