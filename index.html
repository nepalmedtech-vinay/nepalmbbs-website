NMC India — FMGL Regulations, 2021 ✓ Official
📋
NEET Mandatory for All

Per NMC Act 2019 and FMGL Regulations 2021, NEET qualification is mandatory for Indian students wishing to practice medicine in India after completing MBBS abroad. No exceptions.

⏱️
Course Duration — Minimum 54 Months

Minimum 54 months (4.5 years) of academic study + 12 months compulsory internship at the same foreign institution. Total minimum: 5.5 years. Must complete within 10 years of joining.

🌐
English Medium — Mandatory

The medium of instruction must be English. This is a mandatory requirement for foreign medical graduates applying for Indian permanent registration.

🏥
12 Months India Supervised Internship

After completing foreign MBBS + 12-month foreign internship, graduates must additionally complete a minimum 12-month supervised internship in India before applying for permanent registration with NMC.

📝
NExT Exam — Replacing FMGE

Graduates must clear the National Exit Test (NExT) conducted by NBE to obtain permanent registration. NExT is replacing FMGE for all graduates — Indian and foreign. Check NBE for current status.

⚡
Existing Students — Grandfathering

Students who acquired their foreign degree OR were pursuing education before November 2021 are governed by earlier FMGE regulations, not the 2021 NExT regulations.

📚
Mandatory Subjects — Schedule I

Must include: Community Medicine, General Medicine, Psychiatry, Paediatrics, General Surgery, Anaesthesia, OBG, Orthopaedics, ENT, Ophthalmology, Dermatology, Emergency services.

Nepal Medical Council — Official Notice, November 2024 ✓ Official
⏱️
5.5 Years Minimum MBBS Duration

Nepal Medical Council recognises MBBS/BDS undergraduate degrees with a minimum academic duration of 5.5 years, which includes a compulsory 1-year internship.

🌐
English as Primary Medium of Instruction

All medical colleges in Nepal conduct all academic programs in English as the primary medium of instruction — officially confirmed by Nepal NMC notice dated 10 November 2024.

📋
Provisional Registration During Internship

Nepal NMC provides provisional registration to all MBBS/BDS students in Nepal during their internship at teaching hospitals affiliated to their respective medical colleges.

🌍
Foreign Students — Licensing After Graduation

After graduation, foreign nationals are eligible to take the licensing examinations as per NMC Nepal regulations. Upon passing, they obtain a licence equivalent to Nepalese citizens.

✅
No Good Standing Certificate Required

The Nepal NMC licensing procedure does NOT require a good standing certificate from the candidate's home country medical council — simplifying the process for Indian graduates.

Common Questions
Parents & Students — Top Questions
Admission & Eligibility
Is NEET mandatory for Nepal MBBS?
+

Yes. Per NMC India FMGL Regulations 2021, NEET is mandatory for Indian students who wish to practice medicine in India after MBBS abroad. Minimum 50th percentile for General/OBC; 40th percentile for SC/ST. Verify at nmc.org.in

Is a Nepal MBBS degree valid in India?
+

Valid only from NMC India recognised colleges. After graduating, you must clear NExT or FMGE (conducted by NBE) + complete 1-year supervised internship in India to obtain a licence to practice. Always verify your college at NMC College Search before applying.

How long is the MBBS course in Nepal?
+

5.5 years total — 4.5 years academic study + 1 year compulsory internship at affiliated teaching hospital. Same structure as MBBS in India. Confirmed by Nepal Medical Council official notice (November 2024).

When do Nepal MBBS admissions open in 2025?
+

Typically July to October. MEC Nepal publishes official admission notices at entrance.mec.gov.np. Apply as soon as NEET results are declared to avoid missing deadlines.

How do I verify if a Nepal college is NMC India recognised?
+

Search the specific college at NMC India College Search. This is the ONLY authoritative source. Do not rely on college claims or agent assurances — always verify yourself.

Fees & Finance
What is the fee for Nepal MBBS?
+

Fees are regulated by MEC Nepal's official fee ceiling and vary by college. We share current official MEC fee structures during counseling — not estimates. Verify at mec.gov.np/fees. Always pay fees ONLY directly to the college — never to agents.

Can I get an education loan for Nepal MBBS?
+

Indian banks including SBI and Bank of Baroda provide education loans for MBBS at NMC India recognised foreign colleges. The college's NMC recognition letter is typically required. Consult your bank directly — terms and eligibility vary.

Career & Future
What is NExT / FMGE and is it difficult?
+

NExT (National Exit Test) is replacing FMGE for all medical graduates. Conducted by NBE twice yearly. Nepal's India-aligned curriculum provides a good foundation, but serious and dedicated preparation is required. Current info: natboard.edu.in

What internships are required after Nepal MBBS?
+

Two internships required: 1) 1 year in Nepal at affiliated teaching hospital during MBBS (Nepal NMC requirement, counted in the 5.5 years). 2) Additional 1 year supervised internship in India after graduation (NMC India FMGL Regulations 2021). Both are mandatory to practice in India.

Can I do NEET PG after Nepal MBBS?
+

After clearing NExT/FMGE + completing 1-year supervised India internship, you may be eligible for NEET PG. Rules are subject to NMC updates. Always verify current eligibility at nmc.org.in before making decisions.

Life & Safety
Do Indian students need a visa for Nepal?
+

No. Indian citizens do not require a visa for Nepal under the India-Nepal bilateral open border agreement. A valid Indian passport or any government-issued photo ID is sufficient for entry into Nepal.

Is Nepal safe for Indian students?
+

Nepal and India share historically close cultural, diplomatic and economic ties. Indian students are generally well-received. Top medical colleges provide on-campus security and separate hostels for male and female students. We strongly recommend speaking directly with current Indian students at your shortlisted college before finalising your decision.

What is the monthly living cost in Nepal?
+

Approximately ₹15,000–25,000 per month covering hostel, food, transport, and personal expenses. This varies by college location, lifestyle, and individual spending. On-campus hostel living is typically the most affordable option.

What documents do I need to apply?
+

Required: 10th+12th marksheets (notarised), NEET scorecard, valid passport, medical fitness certificate, character certificate from school/college, passport-size photos. Apply via entrance.mec.gov.np.

Life in Nepal — Honest Picture

Realistic information to help students and parents make informed decisions.

🌡️

Weather

Kathmandu: 5–30°C. Pleasant spring/autumn. Cold winters (5–10°C). Monsoon June–August. Similar to North India.

🍛

Food

Indian food widely available in most college hostels. Dal, roti, sabzi available. Indian restaurants in all major cities.

🏠

Hostel

On-campus hostels with separate blocks for male/female students. 24/7 security, Wi-Fi, dining halls at most colleges.

💰

Monthly Cost

Approx ₹15,000–25,000/month covering hostel, food, transport, and personal expenses. Varies by lifestyle.

📱

Connectivity

Indian SIM cards work in Nepal with roaming. Affordable local SIMs available. Good internet in colleges and cities.

👥

Community

Large Indian student community at all major Nepal medical colleges. Cultural events, student associations, peer support.

🚌

Transport

Direct buses from Gorakhpur/Delhi (Sonauli border). Flights Delhi→Kathmandu available. No visa for Indian citizens.

🏥

Healthcare

Good hospitals in Kathmandu and Pokhara. Indian students have access to college hospital facilities during studies.
